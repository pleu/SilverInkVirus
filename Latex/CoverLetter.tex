% There is a global need for improvements on the anti-viral functionality of reusable personal protective equipment (PPE). Current healthcare protocols in the US require that reusable PPE equipment is washed in bleach solution before being reused. Silver is a popular coating material to enhance the anti-microbial properties of fabric. Many silver fabric treatments demonstrate anti-microbial properties; however, these fabric treatments have not been tested against infectious virus and/or after multiple bleach washing cycles, which is necessary for reusable, functional PPE applications. This work studies the durability of the anti-viral performance for reactive silver ink and silver nanoparticles as viable fabric coatings for reusable, functional PPE applications. 

In this paper, we demonstrate a bleach wash durable 
fabric treatment that simultaneously exhibits liquid repellent and anti-viral properties with enveloped virus stocks by using a reactive silver ink and low surface energy polymer. %, even after extended ultrasonic bleach washing. 
The fabric treatment 
exhibits high static contact angles and low contact angle hysteresis with water, even after 300 minutes of ultrasonic bleach washing. Similarly, the fabric treatment shows reductions of infectious virus quantities by about 2 logs compared to controls for enveloped viruses even after bleach washing. The use of reactive silver ink provides for 
better efficacy and durability compared to silver nanoparticles due to the use of reactive molecular silver for more conformal coverage of fabric microfibers and better adhesion.  This study provides insights for improving the wash durability of anti-viral silver fabric treatments and demonstrates a bleach wash durable, repellent anti-viral treatment for reusable, functional PPE applications.
